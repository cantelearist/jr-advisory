1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The brand has one of the best positioning lines in this category —
"Advocacy, not remediation. We carry no hammers and file no invoices for work."
That single contrast does more than the entire homepage of either direct
competitor. The voice is disciplined, restrained, and correctly priced for an
$8M–$20M estate owner.

And then it buries that line below the fold and opens with a conservation-NGO
headline that CONTRADICTS it.

The hero says "hazardous materials REMEDIATION advisory." The next section's
entire thesis is "Advocacy, NOT remediation." A cold visitor reading for five
seconds files the firm as a remediation contractor — the precise failure
category your own FIRST_20_VISITOR_REVIEW.md was built to detect. The strongest
weapon is in the weakest position, and a word in the hero is actively arguing
against the brand.

IF YOU CHANGE ONE THING: lead with advocacy and kill "remediation" from the
hero. Everything else in this document is secondary to that.

3. STRENGTHS (no inflation — these genuinely work)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1. THE CORE POSITIONING LINE IS CATEGORY-DEFINING.
   "Advocacy, not remediation" + "We carry no hammers and file no invoices for
   work. Our only product is judgment applied exclusively on behalf of the
   owner." This is the independent-advisor playbook executed better than the
   people who wrote it. Independent wealth management spends fortunes trying to
   communicate "whose side of the table are they on" — JRA says it in eleven
   words with a physical image (no hammers). Keep this line forever.

2. RESTRAINT MATCHES THE PRICE POINT.
   Short lines, mono-spaced eyebrows, no exclamation marks, no "Contact us
   today!" desperation. Textbook luxury voice — "luxury behaves like it doesn't
   need you." For a buyer with an $8M–$20M property, the calm is itself a
   credential.

3. THE ORIGIN STORY IS THE SINGLE STRONGEST ASSET ON THE SITE.
   "Twice in thirty years, the canyon claimed the ridge" + the specific 1993
   and 2018 burns is earned, concrete, and emotionally legitimate. Specificity
   — real dates, a real place, a real loss — is exactly what trust copy is
   supposed to do, and most firms can only fake it. JRA doesn't have to.

4. "THE TERMS WE DON'T NEGOTIATE" TURNS BOILERPLATE INTO CONVICTION.
   Privacy / Transparency / Concierge are the three most generic words in
   professional services. Framing them as non-negotiable terms makes them feel
   like a spine instead of a checklist. The scarcity line — "six projects
   annually — intentionally. Judgment cannot be scaled" — is a real
   concierge-medicine move and it mostly lands (see weakness #10 for the tweak).

5. THE MICROCOPY IS HONEST AND HUMAN, WHICH IS RARE AND DISARMING.
   "If it isn't a fit, we'll tell you that quickly, and point you somewhere
   useful if we can" and "That email looks off — worth a second glance" build
   more trust than any trust badge. A genuine voice advantage; protect it.

6. THE PRIVATE OFFICE SHOWS INSTEAD OF TELLS.
   The transparency claim is DEMONSTRATED with a concrete artifact — chain of
   custody intact, 0.008 f/cc below the clearance limit, Rule 1403 filed —
   rather than asserted. The right instinct everywhere: proof beats adjectives.


------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. WEAKNESSES, RANKED BY IMPACT
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

#1 — THE HERO HEADLINE CONTRADICTS THE CORE POSITIONING (highest priority)
   The hero subcopy says "hazardous materials REMEDIATION advisory." The
   Practice section's whole thesis is "Advocacy, NOT remediation." You are
   spending your most valuable pixels arguing the opposite of your
   differentiator. A 5-second visitor files you as a remediation contractor —
   the exact failure mode FIRST_20_VISITOR_REVIEW.md is designed to catch.
   FIX: remove "remediation" from the hero entirely. The firm advises owners
   ABOUT remediation; it does not remediate. Use "advisory," "oversight,"
   "owner's counsel."

#2 — THE HERO HEADLINE SAYS NOTHING ABOUT WHAT THE FIRM DOES
   "Protecting The Coast We Call Home" over a coastline photo reads like a land
   trust or a conservation NGO. A cold visitor cannot tell this is
   hazmat/structural advisory in five seconds. The knockout line ("Advocacy,
   not remediation") is one section down.
   FIX: put the differentiator in the hero. Lead with what you are.

#3 — TWO TAGLINES COMPETING FOR THE SAME JOB, NO HIERARCHY
   "Protecting the Coast" and "Advocacy, not remediation" fight each other for
   the role of brand line. You have a knockout punch and you're throwing a jab
   first.
   FIX: pick one primary line (advocacy); demote the place/emotional line to a
   supporting role in the Origin section, where it belongs.

#4 — THE HERO SUBCOPY IS JARGON-DENSE
   "Ultra-discreet hazardous materials remediation advisory and structural
   inspection oversight for coastal estate owners where cost, liability, and
   pressure arrive together" stacks three nominalizations before it says
   anything a human feels. Compare the clean Practice line directly below it —
   same brand, far better writing.
   FIX: shorter, plainer, one idea. (See rewrite bank.)

#5 — "ULTRA-DISCREET" TELLS INSTEAD OF SHOWS
   Truly discreet brands DEMONSTRATE discretion — NDA-protected, no public
   client list, "not filed, not referenced, not discussed." JRA already does
   this beautifully in the Cornerstone. The adjective "ultra-discreet" in the
   hero undercuts the very thing it claims; discretion announced is discretion
   diminished.
   FIX: cut the adjective. Let the NDA / no-client-list facts carry it.

#6 — NAME VS. FOUNDER IDENTITY IS AMBIGUOUS
   The firm is "James Roman Advisory," the founders are "Roman & Stephen." A
   sharp visitor asks: who is James? Is "James Roman" a person, or a
   portmanteau? Unresolved, it reads slightly invented.
   FIX: one deliberate line that resolves the name — quietly, once.

#7 — ORIGIN PRONOUN WHIPLASH
   "Stephen was born in Malibu... Roman spent years overseeing construction...
   both of them needed it." Two first names plus a company called "James Roman"
   makes the reader work to track who's who, in the one section where you most
   want them absorbed in the story, not parsing it.
   FIX: establish both people with a clear noun early (roles, last names, or a
   one-line "the two founders") so the pronouns have stable referents.

#8 — CTA PROLIFERATION DILUTES THE PATH
   Across the page: "View practice," "Request a private consultation," "Access
   private office," "Request consultation," "Inquire." That's five verbs for
   roughly two real actions (inquire / enter portal).
   FIX: standardize one primary CTA verb for inquiry sitewide; make "Access
   private office" the clearly secondary, returning-client action.

#9 — STRONG ON "WHY WE CARE," THIN ON "WHY WE'RE QUALIFIED"
   The Origin earns emotional trust. But an affluent buyer wants BOTH the
   side-of-the-table AND the competence signal — credentials, certifications,
   years, jurisdictions, sample outcomes. Competitors over-index on capability
   lists; JRA under-indexes. You don't need their volume — you need three or
   four hard credibility facts so the discretion doesn't read as thinness.
   FIX: add a small, restrained credibility layer (certs / standards / years /
   jurisdiction) near the Practice or Cornerstone — facts, not adjectives.

#10 — "JUDGMENT CANNOT BE SCALED" CAN READ AS THE FIRM'S LIMITATION
   To a buyer worried about availability, "we don't scale" can sound like "you
   might not get us." Concierge medicine solved this years ago by always
   framing the cap as a PATIENT benefit ("we keep the panel small SO THAT you
   get...").
   FIX: reframe the cap around what the client GETS (access, the founder's
   actual attention), not what the firm WON'T do.

5. COMPETITOR LANGUAGE TEARDOWN — AND THE GAP JRA CAN OWN
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

PEAK PROJECTS
  Lead: "Owner's Representatives for World-Class Projects" / "meticulous,
  empathetic, and proven approach."
  Voice: capability-led, adjective-driven. Verdict: generic — could be any PM
  firm.

MGMT PARTNERS
  Lead: "Los Angeles-based owner's representative and development management
  firm" + a feasibility/underwriting/entitlements/procurement service list.
  Voice: comprehensive, service-catalog. Verdict: undifferentiated competence.

THE PATTERN: competitors lead with CAPABILITY LISTS and the commodity term
"owner's representative." None of them lead with conflict-free ADVOCACY as the
emotional hook. The category sounds like a services menu.

THE OPENING: JRA's "we carry no hammers and file no invoices for work" is a
genuine moat — an emotional, ethical, memorable contrast that no competitor is
making. AND IT'S HIDDEN BELOW THE FOLD. The competitive fix is not new copy;
it's positioning discipline. Move the moat to the front door. The competition
has handed JRA the entire "whose side are you on" territory by default — take
it explicitly.

6. CROSS-INDUSTRY STEALS (with attributed source language)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

INDEPENDENT WEALTH MANAGEMENT
  The whole sector sells "no products to sell," fee-only / fiduciary
  transparency, and "who's really sitting on their side of the table." JRA
  already has the SUBSTANCE ("no invoices for work") — borrow the
  side-of-the-table FRAMING for the hero. It's the most direct articulation of
  advocacy that exists.

CONCIERGE MEDICINE
  "We see a limited number of patients so we can give you..." — the cap is
  ALWAYS framed as the patient's benefit, never the practice's constraint. The
  exact fix for weakness #10. Steal the "...so that you get..." construction.

LUXURY COPY DOCTRINE
  Restraint, withholding, "behaves like it doesn't need you," Rolex's "A crown
  for every achievement" (powerful for what it WITHHOLDS), discretion-as-product
  (the private-jet category never says "discreet" — it just is). JRA is already
  strong here; the lesson is KEEP CUTTING and SHOW discretion, never label it
  (weakness #5).

ANTI-PITCH TRUST SIGNALING (carried across all three)
  The single most persuasive move for a skeptical wealthy buyer is the
  willingness to say "if it isn't a fit, we'll tell you." JRA already does this
  in the contact success state — PROMOTE THAT HONESTY EARLIER, not just
  post-submit. It's the most disarming sentence on the site and it's hidden
  until after someone commits.

7. REWRITE BANK (options, not applied edits)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

HERO HEADLINE — lead with advocacy, drop "remediation"
  1. "One side of the table. Yours."
  2. "We carry no hammers."  (lifts the strongest existing line into the hero)
  3. "Advocacy, not remediation."  (simply promote the line you already have)
  4. "Owner's counsel for when the canyon takes the ridge."  (ties to Origin)
  5. "The only advisor in the room who works for you."

HERO SUBCOPY — plainer, one idea, no jargon stack
  - "Independent advisory for coastal estate owners facing fire, water, mold,
     or hazardous materials. We don't do the work — we make sure the people who
     do are working for you."
  - "When cost, liability, and pressure arrive together, we're the only party
     in the room with no invoice riding on the outcome."

HERO EYEBROW — keep; it's already good
  - "Owner-side advisory · No contractors · No conflicts" is strong. Leave it.

CONCIERGE / SCARCITY LINE — reframe the cap as the client's benefit
  From: "We limit engagements to six projects annually — intentionally.
         Judgment cannot be scaled."
  To:   "Six engagements a year — so the founding partner is on your matter,
         not managing a queue."
  Or:   "We cap at six projects a year so the person you hire is the person who
         shows up."

"ULTRA-DISCREET" — cut, let facts carry it
  - Delete the adjective. The Cornerstone's "not filed, not referenced, not
    discussed" already proves it.

CTAs — standardize
  - Primary (everywhere inquiry is offered): "Request a consultation" — one
    verb, one phrasing.
  - Secondary (returning clients only): "Enter the Private Office" — visually
    subordinate.

PROMOTE THE HONESTY EARLIER
  - Surface a version of "If it isn't a fit, we'll tell you — and point you
    somewhere useful" near the inquiry form BEFORE submission, not only in the
    success state.
