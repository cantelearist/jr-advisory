# Scoring Rubric

Score each dimension **1–5**. Anchor every score to evidence in the deck.

| Score | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| 5 | Exceptional — a clear reason to lean in. |
| 4 | Strong — credible and well-supported. |
| 3 | Adequate — present but unremarkable or partly unproven. |
| 2 | Weak — material gaps or unconvincing. |
| 1 | Absent / disqualifying. |

## The seven dimensions

1. **Problem & market** — Is the problem real, painful, and large? Is the
   market big and reachable, with a credible bottom-up TAM and a beachhead?

2. **Solution & product** — Does the product clearly solve the problem? Is there
   a defensible insight, technical edge, or design advantage?

3. **Traction & validation** — What's actually been proven? Revenue, growth,
   retention, engagement, pipeline, LOIs, or strong pre-revenue signal —
   calibrated to stage.

4. **Business model & unit economics** — Is the path to revenue clear and the
   economics sound (pricing, margin, CAC/LTV/payback where applicable)?

5. **Go-to-market** — Is there a repeatable, affordable path to customers with
   evidence it works?

6. **Team** — Founder-market fit, relevant track record, complementary skills,
   ability to execute and recruit.

7. **The ask & financials** — Is the raise sized to clear milestones, with a
   credible model and sensible use of funds?

Report each as `Dimension — X/5 — one-line justification`, then an overall
weighted read (weight traction and team most heavily at later stages; team,
market, and insight most heavily at pre-seed).

## Red flags (call these out explicitly when present)

- **Top-down-only TAM** ("1% of a $50B market") with no bottom-up build.
- **Hockey-stick projections** with no assumptions or basis in current traction.
- **"No competitors"** or a competition slide that hides the real incumbents.
- **Vanity metrics** (downloads, signups, impressions) standing in for revenue
  or retention.
- **No traction at a stage where it's expected** (e.g., Series A with no
  repeatable revenue).
- **Vague use of funds** ("growth and operations") with no milestones.
- **Cap-table / structure issues** — prior down round, dead equity, founder
  already diluted, convertible overhang.
- **Solo founder in a domain that needs a team**, or a team with no
  founder-market fit.
- **Churn or retention hidden or omitted** when the model depends on it.
- **Regulatory / legal exposure** glossed over.
- **Unclear or non-venture-scale outcome** — a good business that can't return
  a fund.

## Green flags (note these too — analysis isn't only criticism)

- Bottom-up market sizing and a clearly chosen beachhead.
- Real, recent, accelerating traction with cohorts/retention.
- A non-obvious "why now" insight.
- Founder-market fit with prior relevant outcomes.
- Honest competition framing with a genuine, defensible moat.
- A specific ask tied to milestones that de-risk the next round.
