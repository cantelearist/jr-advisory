# Deck Components Checklist

The canonical slides investors expect, roughly in narrative order. For each,
mark **Present / Missing / Weak** and note what "good" looks like.

| # | Slide | What "good" looks like |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | **Title / Cover** | Company name, one-line description, contact, stage. Sets the category instantly. |
| 2 | **Problem** | A painful, specific, frequent problem for a defined customer. Quantified pain ("teams lose X hours / $Y"). Not a thin pretext for the product. |
| 3 | **Solution** | Clear what the product does and why it uniquely solves the problem. Ideally a screenshot/demo, not adjectives. |
| 4 | **Why now** | The shift (tech, regulatory, behavioral) that makes this possible/urgent today. Explains timing. |
| 5 | **Market size** | TAM/SAM/SOM with a **bottom-up** build (customers × ACV), not just a top-down analyst number. Credible, reachable beachhead. |
| 6 | **Product / How it works** | The core mechanic and the "aha." Defensible technical or design insight if any. |
| 7 | **Traction** | The most scrutinized slide. Revenue, growth rate, users, retention, pipeline, LOIs, or strong qualitative signal at pre-revenue. Up-and-to-the-right with real numbers and timeframes. |
| 8 | **Business model** | How money is made: pricing, ACV, sales motion, gross margin. Unit economics (CAC, LTV, payback) where available. |
| 9 | **Go-to-market** | Repeatable path to customers. Channels, CAC, sales cycle. Evidence the motion works, not just a list of tactics. |
| 10 | **Competition** | Honest landscape. A 2x2 or feature matrix that shows real differentiation and a defensible moat. Claiming "no competitors" is a red flag. |
| 11 | **Team** | Why *this* team wins — founder-market fit, relevant wins, complementary skills. Key gaps acknowledged. |
| 12 | **Financials / Projections** | 3–5 year model tied to assumptions you can interrogate. Growth that's ambitious but defensible. |
| 13 | **The ask** | Amount raising, round structure, runway it buys, and concrete milestones it unlocks. |
| 14 | **Use of funds** | Allocation across hiring/product/GTM mapped to the milestones above. |
| 15 | **Vision / Closing** | Where this goes if it works — the venture-scale outcome. |

## Coverage notes

- **Missing ≠ fatal.** A pre-seed deck may legitimately lack financials and
  detailed GTM. Weight by stage.
- **Order matters.** Problem before solution; traction before the ask. A deck
  that leads with the product before establishing the problem usually reads as
  founder-centric rather than customer-centric.
- **Length.** 10–15 slides for the main deck is the norm; anything past ~20 is
  usually unfocused. Dense appendices are fine.
